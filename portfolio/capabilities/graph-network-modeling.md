# Graph and Network Modeling

## Status

Current and past capability.

## What it solves

Institutions, policies, research ecosystems, cities, and service systems are
relationship-heavy. Graph and network modeling makes those relationships visible
and computable.

## Technical work

- Entity extraction and normalization.
- Relationship typing.
- Knowledge graph design.
- Policy and institutional graph modeling.
- Researcher, institution, capability, and opportunity maps.
- City network metrics.
- Recommender and matching systems.

## Graph objects

- person;
- institution;
- project;
- policy;
- SDG or objective;
- city asset;
- service;
- document;
- opportunity;
- capability;
- technology.

## Current applications

- Hadox Scientific Intelligence.
- Digital twin semantic layers.
- Opportunity CRM and partner maps.
- Urban network metrics.
- Public-policy and institutional analysis.

## Past foundations

- Neo4j and graph work for public-sector programs.
- Policy and SDG relationship modeling.
- Enterprise process and integration mapping.
- Recommender experiments.

## Outputs

- Graph schemas.
- Entity maps.
- Relationship matrices.
- Capability maps.
- Partner maps.
- Recommender logic.
- Network indicators.
