# Public-Sector Innovation and Institutional Design

## Status

Current and past capability.

## What it solves

Public-sector technology projects fail when they are treated only as software.
They require institutional diagnosis, governance, evidence, budget logic,
stakeholder roles, legal constraints, data availability, and implementation
cadence. This capability translates public needs into executable technical and
institutional programs.

## Technical work

- Institutional diagnosis.
- Public-sector data assessment.
- Work-package design.
- Legal / administrative function mapping.
- Observatory design.
- Public evidence review.
- Governance and role models.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Implementation roadmaps.

## Current applications

- Government simplification observatory.
- Public research proposal work under anonymized/public-safe framing.
- Development-bank and cooperation opportunity mapping.
- Public digital infrastructure concept notes.
- Local digital twin public authority collaboration.

## Past foundations

- Public policy graph modeling.
- Sustainability and public-sector doctoral work.
- Enterprise/government consulting.
- Innovation laboratory diagnosis.

## Outputs

- Technical concept notes.
- Institutional diagnosis reports.
- Source matrices.
- Work plans.
- Partner messages.
- Proposal structures.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Public dashboards or observatory logic.
