# CL-STARTUP-003 - Hackathon and Innovation-Program Methodology

## Public title

Hackathon, challenge, and applied innovation methodology.

## Role

Organizer, mentor, judge, technical architect, and methodology designer across
multiple programs.

## Problem

Hackathons often create energy but fail to produce reusable knowledge, credible
project pipelines, or implementable outcomes. Organizations need a method that
turns ideas into evidence, prototypes, talent maps, vendor discovery, and
post-event execution routes.

## Technical approach

The methodology treats hackathons as structured innovation systems rather than
events. Depending on the context, the format can become a proposal call,
prototype sprint, technical challenge, startup weekend, internal innovation
pipeline, or public-interest technology program.

## Method components

- Challenge framing.
- Participant and mentor onboarding.
- Technical verticals.
- Sponsor and partner packages.
- Judging criteria.
- Evidence and prototype templates.
- Pitch and demo structure.
- Post-event project pipeline.
- Follow-up contracting or incubation route.

## Representative contexts

- LabLab.ai public hackathon and AI app routes.
- Techstars Startup Weekend AI4ALL CDMX.
- MassChallenge and MIT-related mentoring contexts.
- Corporate innovation and internal hackathon proposals.
- Public-sector and university innovation routes.
