# Technical Portfolio

This portfolio is the public, technical layer behind my CV.

It is designed to show capabilities, products, research lines, software systems,
and applied work without exposing client names, confidential documents, or
private implementation details. Where useful, projects use anonymized IDs so the
public record can remain readable while my private records preserve the exact
client or partner context.

## Why this exists

My work does not fit cleanly into a conventional CV. It combines physics,
complex systems, artificial intelligence, digital twins, public policy,
scientific intelligence, software engineering, public-sector consulting,
innovation programs, and research operations.

The purpose of this portfolio is to make that work legible:

- what I build;
- what technical problems I can solve;
- what research lines connect the work;
- what products and prototypes exist;
- what evidence, software, and methodologies support the claims;
- how applied projects connect to academic research and public-interest impact.

## Public / private boundary

This repository can include:

- public repositories and websites;
- anonymized project summaries;
- technical architecture summaries;
- methodological notes;
- open-source or public-facing software outputs;
- non-confidential descriptions of proposals, prototypes, and research systems.

This repository must not include:

- client names when the project is not public;
- confidential files, contracts, budgets, credentials, or personal data;
- private email threads or partner-sensitive notes;
- unredacted government or company documents;
- the private client-ID mapping.

The private map from client IDs to real organizations should live outside Git or
inside `private/`, which is ignored by Git.

## Editorial principle

This is not a marketing portfolio. Each page should answer technical questions:

- What problem was modeled?
- What data existed and what data was missing?
- What architecture or method was used?
- What was built, tested, or delivered?
- What can be reused in another city, institution, or research project?
- What remains private, uncertain, or still under development?

The public version should be technically dense enough to be useful, while still
protecting private clients and partners.
