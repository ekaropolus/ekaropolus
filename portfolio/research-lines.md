# Research Lines

## Open Local Digital Twins

Reusable digital twin infrastructure for cities and territories.

Core questions:

- How can a city move from fragmented public data to a computable operating layer?
- How can local digital twins remain open, inspectable, and reusable?
- How can semantic city layers connect buildings, roads, public services,
  people-flow, climate, energy, waste, planning, and institutional decisions?
- How can digital twins support decisions without becoming opaque dashboards?

Representative work:

- Polisplexity Local Digital Twin architecture.
- Twin Base Studio and open local digital twin repository work.
- City-fragment XR / 3D prototypes for walk, compare, and overlay modes.
- European local digital twin call preparation and partner intelligence.
- Energy, waste, planning, and resilience add-on concepts.

## Scientific Intelligence Infrastructure

Systems that convert fragmented scientific, technical, institutional, and
venture evidence into reusable decision support.

Core questions:

- How can institutions preserve source-grounded evidence while using AI?
- How can papers, datasets, PDFs, spreadsheets, inboxes, and expert memory become
  structured intelligence assets?
- How can AI assistants cite sources, expose uncertainty, and support human
  decision-makers instead of replacing them?

Representative work:

- Hadox Scientific Intelligence platform direction.
- Mexico / Netherlands / Belgium scientific intelligence surfaces.
- EVA Breast Cancer Archive Platform.
- ACE Electoral Intelligence migration and knowledge-system plan.
- Codex Worklog as an internal research-operations platform.

## Urban Scaling, SAMI, and Multiscale City Methods

Research software and methodology for urban scaling, support units, and
reproducible city-analysis workflows.

Core questions:

- What is the correct unit of analysis for urban scaling?
- How do results change across city, state, AGEB, manzana, hex, or geofence supports?
- Can digital twin outputs be replayed and tested outside the production app?
- Can a monograph be mapped to executable code and database artifacts?

Representative work:

- `urban-sami`: headless SAMI workflow engine.
- Monograph-to-code crosswalk.
- Equation traceability.
- Polisplexity parity workflows with mismatch checks.
- INEGI, DENUE, OSM, PostGIS, and city-network experiments.

## Civic AI and Public-Sector Decision Systems

AI systems for public-interest institutions where traceability, responsibility,
and human review matter.

Core questions:

- How can AI help public servants, researchers, and analysts handle large evidence
  volumes without losing accountability?
- What should an AI agent do in a public-sector workflow?
- How do we move from documents to decisions while keeping source lineage?

Representative work:

- Public-policy graph models.
- Electoral intelligence systems.
- Government simplification observatories.
- AI-assisted sector research and fintech intelligence.
- Local digital twin decision workflows.

## High-Performance Computing and Advanced Modeling

Computational infrastructure for data-intensive modeling, simulation, and
territorial analysis.

Core questions:

- Which digital twin workloads benefit from HPC or parallel processing?
- How do tiling, OSM extraction, spatial preprocessing, and batch simulations
  move from a workstation to a supercomputer or VPS cluster?
- What does regional HPC adoption require in terms of talent, business model,
  and institutional coordination?

Representative work:

- `CL-GOV-003` HPC diagnostic and business-model work.
- OpenMPI learning and local/remote processing comparison.
- Quantum technologies national strategy research compendium.
