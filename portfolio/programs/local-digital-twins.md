# Local Digital Twins Program

## Public framing

This program documents the work behind Polisplexity, Twin Base Studio, and the
European Local Digital Twin opportunity pipeline. It is broader than a single
proposal or product. It combines product architecture, city use-case design,
technical due diligence, partner intelligence, public-funding strategy, and
operational delivery planning.

## Why this matters

Local Digital Twins are not useful when they are treated as static 3D viewers.
The relevant problem is to connect city evidence, source systems, semantic
meaning, scenarios, and public-service decisions in a way that a city can
operate, verify, and reuse.

The working thesis is:

```text
city sources -> governed inventory -> semantic meaning -> scenarios
  -> municipal service workflows -> evidence for human decisions
```

## City and use-case architecture

The active technical design has been structured around multiple city profiles
and reusable service families rather than a single generic demonstration.

### Tallinn

Tallinn has been treated as the most technically mature route: digital
construction, planning workflows, GIS/BIM/IFC, model validation, city-system
integration, and authority-grade local administration of a twin instance.

Relevant service angles:

- territorial planning and urban intervention review;
- construction/digital-building data alignment;
- BIM/IFC ingestion and model anchoring;
- planning evidence for municipal review;
- integration with existing advanced municipal digital infrastructure.

### Kharkiv

Kharkiv has been framed around reconstruction, resilience, housing, communal
services, data integration, restricted-data handling, and staged recovery
planning.

Relevant service angles:

- reconstruction and service-continuity evidence;
- housing, infrastructure, and public-service recovery scenarios;
- restricted-data deployment patterns;
- secure local or hybrid processing;
- resilience and vulnerability analysis.

### Gaziantep

Gaziantep has been considered as a potential planning and resilience route,
especially where GIS/BIM, disasters, heat, earthquakes, infrastructure, traffic,
and regional data platforms can be connected into actionable municipal services.

Relevant service angles:

- urban planning with GIS/BIM;
- disaster preparedness and heat-risk scenarios;
- mobility and traffic-related service intelligence;
- infrastructure and public-lighting data;
- continuity-of-services analysis.

## Technology evaluated and positioned

The technology surface has included both implemented runtime components and
proposal-level integration targets.

### Implemented or scaffolded technical base

- PostgreSQL/PostGIS as spatial source of truth;
- Docker-based deployment;
- authenticated city workspace;
- city registry and multi-city configuration;
- provider, source, and layer ingestion registry;
- 2D map surface using modern web mapping tooling;
- 3D and immersive viewer routes;
- BIM/IFC metadata and asset handling experiments;
- source artifacts, cached payloads, and ingestion logs;
- semantic packs and scenario-contract scaffolding;
- report/evidence generation patterns.

### Data and connector families

- GeoJSON;
- CSV with coordinate or geometry columns;
- OGC API Features / WFS GeoJSON;
- STAC footprints;
- CityJSON centroids;
- Shapefile and GeoPackage package jobs;
- raster COG and WMS metadata inspection;
- HTTP JSON and sensor-feed summaries;
- MQTT registration metadata;
- 3D Tiles metadata;
- IFC/BIM package handling.

### Standards and interoperability directions

- DCAT / DCAT-AP-style catalog publication;
- JSON-LD and RDF-ready semantic descriptions;
- NGSI-LD and Smart Data Models projections;
- FIWARE-compatible context-broker scaffolding;
- OGC API Features;
- ODRL-style policy metadata;
- EU data-space and LDT Toolbox alignment;
- MIMs Plus interpretation for local digital twin maturity.

### AI and scenario capabilities

- AI-enabled dataset inspection and field mapping;
- provenance and missing-data checks;
- semantic-pack binding drafts;
- standards-output checks;
- scenario-contract preparation;
- municipal evidence briefs;
- human-in-the-loop review gates;
- predictive, prospective, or prescriptive scenario framing.

## What this demonstrates

- Ability to turn an emerging product into a funding-ready technical program.
- Ability to translate European call rules into executable architecture.
- Ability to separate public narrative, private diligence, and legal eligibility.
- Ability to design multi-city pilots without pretending that all cities have
  the same digital maturity.
- Ability to map GIS, BIM, standards, data spaces, AI agents, and service
  workflows into one coherent local digital twin delivery model.
- Ability to prepare a reusable evidence base for future digital twin, smart
  city, civic AI, resilience, and public-infrastructure work.
